EASY WEEKNIGHT SPAGHETTI RECIPE

INGREDIENTS:
- 1 pound spaghetti noodles
- 1 pound ground beef (or ground turkey/chicken)
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried basil
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 tablespoon sugar (to balance acidity)
- Parmesan cheese for serving
- Fresh parsley or basil for garnish

INSTRUCTIONS:

1. COOK THE PASTA:
   - Bring a large pot of salted water to boil (1 tablespoon salt per 4 quarts water)
   - Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente (usually 8-12 minutes)
   - Drain and set aside

2. MAKE THE MEAT SAUCE:
   - Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat
   - Add ground beef and cook until browned (8-10 minutes), breaking it into small pieces
   - Add diced onions and cook until softened (about 5 minutes)
   - Add minced garlic, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes
   - Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant

3. ADD TOMATOES:
   - Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute
   - Add crushed tomatoes and sugar
   - Season with salt and pepper to taste
   - Bring to a simmer

4. SIMMER:
   - Reduce heat to low and let sauce simmer for 25-30 minutes
   - Stir occasionally to prevent sticking

5. COMBINE AND SERVE:
   - Toss cooked spaghetti with the meat sauce
   - Top with freshly grated Parmesan cheese
   - Garnish with fresh herbs

COOKING TIME: 45 minutes total
SERVES: 4-6 people

TIPS:
- Salt your pasta water well - it should taste like the sea
- Cook pasta 1-2 minutes less than package directions for perfect al dente
- You can use any ground meat (beef, turkey, chicken, or pork)
- Store-bought marinara sauce works too if you're short on time
- Leftovers can be refrigerated for 3-4 days or frozen for up to 3 months
